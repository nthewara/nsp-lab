# Q3 FY25 Financial Summary — Acme Cloud Co.

## Executive summary

Q3 FY25 was a strong quarter for Acme Cloud Co. We posted **total revenue of $4.2M**, up 18% year-over-year. Cloud infrastructure remains our growth engine, but the **AI Services** line tripled QoQ on the back of two enterprise design wins. Gross margin held at 71% despite ongoing investment in our Sydney region build-out.

## Headline numbers

| Metric                        | Q3 FY25 | Q2 FY25 | Q3 FY24 | YoY    |
|-------------------------------|---------|---------|---------|--------|
| Revenue                       | $4.20M  | $3.65M  | $3.55M  | +18.3% |
| Gross margin                  | 71%     | 70%     | 69%     | +2pp   |
| Operating expenses            | $2.55M  | $2.30M  | $2.15M  | +18.6% |
| Operating income              | $0.43M  | $0.25M  | $0.30M  | +43.3% |
| Free cash flow                | $0.61M  | $0.18M  | $0.41M  | +48.8% |
| Net new logos                 | 38      | 32      | 27      | +40.7% |
| Net revenue retention (NRR)   | 121%    | 118%    | 115%    | +6pp   |

## Revenue by product line

- **Cloud Infrastructure (compute, storage, networking): $2.30M (55%)** — flat QoQ, growing slowly as our largest customers optimise spend.
- **AI Services (model hosting, fine-tuning, RAG): $0.85M (20%)** — up from $0.28M in Q2; two large logos signed annual contracts.
- **Managed Security (NSP, WAF, log analytics): $0.62M (15%)** — strong attach rate to infra, +22% QoQ.
- **Professional Services (consulting, migrations): $0.43M (10%)** — lumpy as usual; one large migration recognised in March.

## Revenue by region

- **APAC: $1.85M (44%)** — Australia is now our #1 country by revenue; Sydney region build-out paying off.
- **North America: $1.55M (37%)** — steady; one churn offset by two enterprise expansions.
- **EMEA: $0.55M (13%)** — soft quarter; UK pipeline slipping into Q4.
- **LATAM: $0.25M (6%)** — small but doubling YoY.

## Top three deals

1. **Northwind Insurance** — three-year, $480k annual, AI Services + Managed Security. Closed late January.
2. **Contoso Logistics** — two-year, $260k annual, expanding cloud infra to Sydney.
3. **Fabrikam Manufacturing** — one-year, $190k, brand-new logo, AI knowledge-base build.

## Costs & investments

- Sydney region (`australiaeast`) capacity expansion: $310k capex landed in Q3, on schedule.
- Headcount: 84 → 91 (net +7 — mostly cloud SREs and AI solution architects).
- R&D as % of revenue: 22% (target 20–25%).
- Marketing as % of revenue: 11% (down from 13% in Q2 as field events tapered).

## Customer success

- NRR 121%; gross retention 96%.
- Top three churn reasons: budget cuts (50%), missing feature parity with AWS in one niche (33%), M&A-driven consolidation (17%).

## Risks & headwinds

- AOAI GPU capacity in `australiaeast` remains tight; we're queuing customer onboardings.
- AI model pricing pressure from larger hyperscalers — keeping our AI Services margin in the high 50s instead of our target 65%.
- FX: weak AUD shaved ~$70k off USD-reported revenue.

## Q4 FY25 guidance

- Revenue: **$4.5–4.7M** (mid-point +9% QoQ).
- Gross margin: 70–72%.
- Operating income: $0.5–0.6M.
- Net new logos: 35–45.

We expect AI Services to surpass Managed Security as our #3 line, and we are reaffirming the FY25 full-year guidance of $15.5–16.0M revenue and 70%+ gross margin.

## Glossary

- **NRR**: Net revenue retention — revenue from existing customers in the current period relative to revenue from those same customers in the prior-year period, including upgrades and downgrades.
- **NSP**: Network Security Perimeter — Azure boundary service used to isolate this lab's resources.
